How TARA Works

TARA is a roadside safety system built to protect workers, improve awareness, support emergency response, and provide towing-specific AI guidance in one place.

This page explains what each function does, how the system behaves, and what the user should expect when using it in the field.

What TARA Is

TARA is not just a chat tool. It is a roadside safety platform designed for tow operators and roadside workers who may be exposed to traffic, impact risk, and fast-changing danger at the scene.

TARA combines emergency activation, worker protection, hazard awareness, and towing-focused AI support into one mobile system.

🚨

Emergency Button

The emergency button is a hold-to-activate feature. When held for 3 seconds, TARA starts an emergency countdown, gives the user a chance to cancel, and then sends an alert with GPS location if not stopped in time.

🦺

Roadside Protection

Roadside Protection is designed for workers who are outside the truck and actively exposed to roadside danger. It watches for impact or unsafe no-response situations, starts a driver check, and escalates to emergency only if the user does not respond.

⚠️

Hazard Watch

Hazard Watch listens for abnormal nearby danger and warns the worker. It is meant to improve awareness on scene. It does not trigger emergency alerts on its own. It is a warning layer, not an emergency layer.

🤖

Towing AI Assistant

TARA includes a towing and roadside safety AI assistant that answers towing questions, supports field decision-making, and provides practical safety-focused guidance based on the situation.

System Behavior

1

Emergency Activation

If the emergency button is held for 3 seconds, TARA starts a visible emergency countdown, plays an alarm, and gives the user a cancel option.

2

Roadside Protection Check

When Roadside Protection is active, TARA watches for impact and worker-response conditions. If a serious event is detected, it starts a driver check before escalating further.

3

Hazard Watch Warning

When Hazard Watch is active, TARA listens for abnormal nearby danger and warns the worker. This warning is intended to improve awareness and does not go directly into emergency alerting.

4

Emergency Alert and GPS

If the user does not respond during an active emergency check, TARA sends an emergency alert with available GPS location information.

5

Fail-Safe Queue

If delivery cannot happen immediately, TARA can queue the alert and retry later when connection becomes available again.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is TARA?

TARA is a towing and roadside safety system that combines emergency protection, worker monitoring, hazard awareness, and AI support into one mobile platform.

What does the emergency button do?

It starts a hold-to-activate emergency sequence. If the user does not cancel the countdown, TARA sends an emergency alert and GPS location to the configured contact system.

What is Roadside Protection?

Roadside Protection is the worker protection mode. It is meant for use when the operator is out on scene and exposed to roadside risk. It can detect possible impact events and run a driver check before emergency escalation.

What is Hazard Watch?

Hazard Watch is a warning-only awareness feature. It listens for abnormal nearby danger and warns the worker, but it does not automatically send emergency alerts.

Should Hazard Watch be used by itself?

It can be used by itself, but it works best with Roadside Protection turned on. Hazard Watch improves awareness. Roadside Protection adds worker-response and emergency escalation.

Does TARA send GPS location?

Yes. During emergency activation, TARA can send available GPS location data as part of the alert process.

What happens if there is weak signal?

TARA includes fail-safe queue logic. If an alert cannot be delivered immediately, it can remain queued and retry when connection becomes available.

Does Hazard Watch trigger emergency automatically?

No. Hazard Watch is designed to warn the worker only. Emergency escalation is handled through the emergency flow and Roadside Protection logic.

Can TARA answer general questions?

TARA is designed to stay focused on towing, roadside work, and safety-related topics.

Why is this useful for towing companies?

TARA helps improve worker protection, increases roadside awareness, supports emergency response, and gives operators access to safety-focused towing guidance in the field.

What TARA Is Designed To Do

  • Protect workers who are exposed on roadside calls
  • Warn the worker of possible nearby danger
  • Support emergency alerting with location
  • Provide towing-specific AI support and guidance
  • Help companies improve field safety and response readiness
